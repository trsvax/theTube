> [!TLDR]
> Ordered a Radica Rover truck topper. Ships in 3–5 weeks. The plan: drive to every national park in the lower 48 and sleep in the back.

The thing that sold me on the [Radica Rover](https://www.radicaproducts.com/pages/rover) over a standard ARE or Leer shell wasn't the aluminum frame or the honeycomb roof or even the integrated rack. It was this: on a full-size F-150, you can sleep sideways.

Most toppers turn the bed into a long narrow coffin. You're sleeping head-to-tail with the truck, feet at the tailgate, head at the cab wall. The Rover on a full-size bed is wide enough to flip 90 degrees. That's a real bed. That's the difference between a camping trip and a road trip.

> [!GEAR]
> Radica Rover, full-size 5.5' bed, with windows. Starting at $4,099. Ships in 3–5 weeks.

The plan is the lower 48 national parks. Not all at once — this is a project, not a sprint. But the Rover makes it possible to show up somewhere and just stop. No reservation. No campsite booking three months out. Pull off, drop the tailgate, sleep.

The truck handles the miles. The Rover handles the nights. Now I wait.
